EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
Two foundational macro views.
THESIS A — REGIME SHIFT
The post-war monetary and institutional order is entering a period of binding constraint. Sovereign debt, entitlement obligations, and political incentives all bias outcomes toward financial repression — the slow, systematic transfer of value from nominal claims to real assets.
THESIS B — AI INFRASTRUCTURE
AI is the commoditization of cognitive labor and the most capital-intensive infrastructure buildout in history. The binding constraints are physical — power generation, advanced packaging, cooling, rare earth minerals, and land.
THE INTERSECTION
These aren’t parallel trends — they’re reinforcing. AI capex is creating massive new demand for the exact commodities and infrastructure that fiscal constraint makes harder to supply. Energy, copper, land, and industrial capacity sit at the convergence of both forces. The portfolio that captures one captures both.
Why now?
Correlation regime has shifted
Bonds and equities now fall together. Traditional diversification is structurally broken.
AI capex past the point of no return
Hyperscalers are locked into multi-year build commitments. The physical demand curve is set.
Central banks already moving
Record gold accumulation signals the regime shift is no longer speculative — it’s institutional.
Supply pipelines are years out
New mines, power plants, and datacenters take 5–7 years. The scarcity window is open now.
